# Dynatrace style — top 10 tips

Quick-reference checklist covering the most impactful writing rules.
Source: styleguide.dynatrace.com/docs/best-practices/top-10-tips

---

## 1. Use plain language
Write for a global audience. Choose common words over academic or specialised
ones. Replace jargon with terms your readers already know.

## 2. Use active voice
The subject performs the action. Active voice is more direct, concise, and
engaging than passive voice.
- ✅ Dynatrace identifies the root cause.
- ❌ The root cause is identified by Dynatrace.

## 3. Be useful — add value
Focus on what the reader needs to know or do. Remove filler phrases like
"it should be noted that" and "in order to."

## 4. Write scannable content
Use headings, bulleted lists, numbered lists, and tables to break up large
blocks of text. Front-load sentences with keywords.

## 5. Lead with value
Put key information in the first paragraph. Write like a journalist — summary
first, details second.

## 6. Use American English
- behavior (not behaviour), center (not centre), color (not colour)
- catalog (not catalogue), favorite (not favourite)

## 7. Be concise
Remove unnecessary words. If you can say it in 10 words instead of 20, do.
- ✅ "To install…" — ❌ "In order to install…"
- ✅ "Use" — ❌ "Utilize"

## 8. Be consistent
Use the same term for the same concept throughout a document. Don't alternate
between synonyms (e.g., "environment" vs. "tenant" vs. "instance").

## 9. Use serial commas and avoid ampersands
- ✅ Infrastructure Monitoring, Log Monitoring, and Application Security
- ❌ Infrastructure Monitoring, Log Monitoring & Application Security

## 10. Use sentence-case capitalization
Capitalise the first word and proper nouns only — in titles, headings,
labels, and list items.
- ✅ How Dynatrace boosts production resilience
- ❌ How Dynatrace Boosts Production Resilience

---

## Bonus rules (from AEO guidelines)

- **Front-load keywords** in sentences and headings for search optimisation.
- **No gerund (-ing) verb forms** in headings: "Create" not "Creating."
- **Use parallel writing structures** in lists and headings (all action verbs
  or all noun phrases, not a mix).
- **Use FAQ-style headings** where appropriate: "What is Smartscape?"
- **Include one idea per paragraph.**
