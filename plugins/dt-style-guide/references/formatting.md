# Dynatrace formatting rules

Numbers, punctuation, lists, titles/headings, acronyms, dates, link text, and emoji.
Sources: styleguide.dynatrace.com/docs/style/numbers, punctuation, lists,
titles-and-headings, acronyms, date-and-time, link-text, emoji

---

## Titles and headings

### Sentence-case capitalization (always)
Capitalise the first word and proper nouns only. Everything else is lowercase.

- ✅ The DPS license model allows broad adoption of Infrastructure Monitoring
- ❌ The DPS License Model Allows Broad Adoption of Infrastructure Monitoring
- ✅ How Dynatrace boosts production resilience with Site Reliability Guardian
- ❌ How Dynatrace Boosts Production Resilience with Site Reliability Guardian

### No closing punctuation in headings
- ✅ Precise customer communications improve engagement
- ❌ Precise customer communications improve engagement.
- Exception: question marks for FAQ-style headings are acceptable.

### No gerund (-ing) verb forms in headings
- ✅ Create an anomaly detector
- ❌ Creating an anomaly detector
- ✅ Investigate and compare problems
- ❌ Investigating and comparing problems

### Subtitles with colon
- ✅ Stay ahead of the game: Forecast IT capacity with Dynatrace Grail
- ❌ Stay ahead of the game—Forecast IT capacity with Dynatrace Grail
- Capitalise the first word of the subtitle after the colon.

### Heading hierarchy
- H1 = page title, H2 = sections, H3 = subsections, H4 = sub-subsections
- Include two or more H2 headings per page.

## Numbers

### In sentences
- Spell out zero through nine: "The company uses two data centers."
- Use numerals for 10+: "The host group contains 11 hosts."
- Never begin a sentence with a numeral: rewrite or spell it out.
- ✅ Twelve processes run on the host. ❌ 12 processes run on the host.
- ✅ More than 10 processes run. ❌ 10 or more processes run.

### In titles/headings
- Numerals are acceptable, including 0–9: "5 top tips for DevOps success"

### In measurements/currency
- Always use numerals: 8 ft, $100, 3.61 lb, 1 cm, 75°F
- Insert space between numeral and unit: "100 ms" not "100ms"
- No space before %, $, °: "$100", "100%", "75°F"

### Thousands separator
- Use commas for 4+ digits: 3,450 — 2,200 MB
- No commas in addresses, page numbers, or after decimal points.

### Round numbers ≥ 1 million
- Use numeral + modifier: "8 billion dollars", "$3 million per year"

## Punctuation

### Serial comma (always)
- ✅ Infrastructure Monitoring, Log Monitoring, and Application Security.
- ❌ Infrastructure Monitoring, Log Monitoring and Application Security.

### Commas after introductory phrases
- ✅ With Dynatrace, you get precise answers from data.
- ❌ With Dynatrace you get precise answers from data.

### No comma in compound predicates
- ✅ Davis AI evaluates transactions and alerts you when anomalies are detected.
- ❌ Davis AI evaluates transactions, and alerts you when anomalies are detected.
- (But comma is correct when two independent clauses have different subjects.)

### Ampersand (&)
- ❌ Support & services → ✅ Support and services
- Exception: space-constrained UI only.

### em dash (—)
- No spaces around em dashes.
- ✅ While processes are host centric—usually only associated with a single machine—services can span multiple machines.
- ❌ processes are host centric — usually only associated with a single machine — services can span multiple machines.

### Parentheses for acronyms
- ✅ Dynatrace Platform Subscription (DPS)
- ❌ DPS (Dynatrace platform subscription)

---

## Acronyms

- Write the full term first, followed by the acronym in parentheses.
- Don't capitalise all initial letters when defining an acronym unless they
  are proper nouns.
- Don't define acronyms in headings.
- ✅ software-defined networking (SDN)
- ❌ SDN (Software-Defined Networking)

---

## Lists

### Bulleted lists
- If items complete the introductory phrase → colon after intro, period after each item.
- If all items ≤ 3 words → no periods.
- If items are standalone sentences → period after items, no colon after intro.
- If items don't complete the intro → no colon.

### Numbered lists
- Use for sequential procedures or ranked items.
- Each item is a complete sentence with a period.

## Dates and times

### Dates
- Format: month day, year → "January 31, 2023"
- ❌ 31 January 2023, 1/31/23
- ISO format for numerical dates only: YYYY-MM-DD
- No ordinals in dates: ❌ Feb 21st

### Times
- 12-hour clock with AM/PM: "10:45 AM", "2:30 PM"
- ❌ 14:30, 10:45 (24-hour without context)
- Include time zone when relevant: "10:45 AM Pacific Time"

### Seasons
- Avoid season references (they vary by hemisphere).
- ✅ Q1 2024 — ❌ this winter

---

## Link text

- Link text must be descriptive — include words from the target page title.
- ❌ "here," "this page," "link," "more" as link text.
- Use sentence-case in link text.
- Don't include closing punctuation in link text.
- Use approved interaction verbs: "go to" (required content), "visit" (optional),
  "see" / "see also" (reference).

---

## Emoji

- ❌ No emoji in product UI, documentation, website copy, or blog posts.
- Use words to convey feeling; use Dynatrace-standard icons for visual interest.
- Emoji are acceptable only in internal communications (use judgment) and when
  a customer/partner uses one first.
